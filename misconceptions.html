Importance & Misconceptions

Exercise science is vital for understanding the systems that help the human body move, adapt, and perform. This knowledge allows athletes to perform at the highest levels and recover more effectively from injuries, and it helps weightlifters perform incredible feats of strength while safely reaching their maximum strength potential. However, exercise science is not only for elite athletes. It is also essential for keeping people healthy and active throughout their lives, many studies are specifically designed around many populations, even older populations. Studies show that regular exercise helps maintain bone density, muscle mass, balance, and overall health, reducing the risk of injury, falls, and chronic disease.

Myth vs Reality

Exercise science is a relatively new field and is often criticized for not being a “real” science like other specialties. Research in this area can be difficult to control because there are many variables and outliers that cannot be fully accounted for. Each person’s body, habits, training history, and lifestyle are different, which makes standardized studies challenging. However, exercise science is built on extensive research in muscle physiology and biomechanics, which form a strong scientific foundation for the field by explaining how muscles generate force, how joints move under load, and how the body adapts to training and stress over time. These principles are rooted in established biological and physical sciences, reinforcing exercise science as a legitimate and evidence-based discipline.. .
